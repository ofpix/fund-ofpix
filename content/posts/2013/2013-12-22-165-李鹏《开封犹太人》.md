## 作品简介

### 开封犹太人

一千多年以前，两河流域的一支犹太人经由丝绸之路抵达当时的北宋都城开封。他们受到了宋王朝统治者的欢迎，被允许在宋朝经商、与宋人通婚，并授予官职，甚至赐其中一部分人“赵”姓。开封犹太社区在鼎盛时期有将近5000人，他们拥有自己的犹太会堂、拉比和一些办事机构。直到今日，生活在开封的犹太后裔仍有500人以上，他们分为“七姓八家”，分散在开封市内。近些年来，这些犹太人后裔开始寻找自己的根源，尤其是他们中的年轻人希望恢复他们原有的犹太社区生活。

### Kaifeng's Jewish

Over a thousand years ago, the first Jews from Persia traveled through the Silk Road and finally arrived in Kaifeng, which was one of the ancient capitals of imperial China. They were warmly welcomed at that period of time, and one of the Jewish families has even been granted the Emperor’s family name, Zhao.At its height, in the Middle Ages, Kaifeng’s Jewish community numbered as many as 5,000 people, with rabbis, synagogues and various communal institutions. Until today, however, there are between 500 and 1,000 identifiable descendants of the Jewish community, and in recent years an awakening has been taking place among them, as increasing numbers of young Kaifeng Jews.
